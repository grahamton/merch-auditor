# Audit Rubrics & Heuristics

Detailed scoring guidance for each dimension of the storefront audit framework.

---

## UX & Design Quality — Detailed Rubric

### Above the Fold (Homepage)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Hero communicates brand, product category, and primary CTA in under 5 seconds. Image or video directly shows the product in use. Mobile version maintains hierarchy. |
| 7–8 | Hero is clear but CTA is slightly buried or image is generic stock photo. Mobile acceptable but not optimized. |
| 5–6 | Takes 5–10 seconds to understand what the brand sells. CTA present but weak placement. |
| 1–4 | Unclear what the brand sells above the fold. CTA absent or invisible. |

### Navigation
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Max 7 top-level nav items. Mega menu (if present) is scannable. Search is visible. Mobile uses hamburger with accessible tap targets (min 44px). |
| 7–8 | Nav works but some items are redundant or oddly named. Mobile functional. |
| 5–6 | Navigation requires thought. Some items hidden or miscategorized. |
| 1–4 | User must hunt to find core categories. Navigation is confusing or broken on mobile. |

### Design Consistency
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Consistent type scale, color palette, button styles. Feels like a single designer made every page. |
| 7–8 | Mostly consistent with 1–2 outlier pages or sections. |
| 5–6 | Noticeable inconsistencies across pages (different button styles, font sizes). |
| 1–4 | Design system is absent. Looks like multiple uncoordinated templates. |

## Conversion Optimization — Detailed Rubric

### Trust Signal Checklist
Award points for each present and prominent:
- [ ] Star rating + review count visible on product page
- [ ] Real customer photos (not just brand photos)
- [ ] Money-back or satisfaction guarantee stated clearly
- [ ] Security badge near checkout
- [ ] Return/refund policy linked near CTA
- [ ] "As seen in" press logos (if applicable)
- [ ] Verified purchase labels on reviews

**8+ signals present**: 9–10 | **5–7**: 7–8 | **3–4**: 5–6 | **0–2**: 1–4

### CTA Effectiveness
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Primary CTA uses specific action verb ("Get My Kit", "Add to Cart"), high contrast color, above fold on both desktop and mobile. No competing CTAs on same screen. |
| 7–8 | CTA is clear but uses generic copy ("Shop Now") or placement is slightly below fold. |
| 5–6 | CTA present but competes with secondary actions or is low contrast. |
| 1–4 | CTA is hard to find, uses passive language, or is absent on key pages. |

### Pricing Transparency
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Full cost (including shipping estimate) visible before checkout. Discount shown as both original price and percentage. Subscription pricing clearly labeled. |
| 7–8 | Pricing clear but shipping cost only revealed at cart stage. |
| 5–6 | Pricing present but confusing (e.g., obscure tiered pricing without clear explanation). |
| 1–4 | Hidden costs appear at checkout. Surprise fees. |

## Copywriting & Messaging — Detailed Rubric

### 5-Second Homepage Test
Ask: "After 5 seconds on this page, can a new visitor answer: What do they sell? Who is it for? Why buy here?"

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | All three questions answered clearly above the fold. |
| 7–8 | Product and audience clear, but "why here" requires scrolling. |
| 5–6 | Product category clear but niche audience and differentiation absent. |
| 1–4 | Even the product category is unclear in 5 seconds. |

### Benefit vs. Feature Analysis
Sample product description copy and count:
- Feature statements: "Made with 100% cotton" / "12-hour battery life"
- Benefit statements: "Stays soft wash after wash" / "Get through the whole workday on a single charge"

**Ideal ratio**: At least 60% benefit-framed statements.

### Tone Consistency Check
Read copy from: Homepage headline → About page → Product description → CTA buttons → Error messages (if accessible).

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Unmistakable brand voice consistent across all touchpoints. Sounds like one person wrote everything. |
| 7–8 | Consistent on main pages, slightly generic on product pages or support copy. |
| 5–6 | Clear tone on homepage but generic/corporate elsewhere. |
| 1–4 | Brand voice absent or contradictory across pages. |

## Competitive Positioning — Detailed Rubric

### USP Visibility Test
Does the unique selling proposition appear:
- In the hero headline or subheadline? (+3)
- In the About section? (+2)
- Repeated in product descriptions? (+2)
- In email/SMS capture copy? (+1)
- Absent entirely? (0)

**8–10 points**: 9–10 | **5–7**: 7–8 | **3–4**: 5–6 | **0–2**: 1–4

### Brand-Price Alignment
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Design, photography, copy tone, and pricing all signal the same tier (premium/mid/value). Cohesive brand world. |
| 7–8 | Mostly aligned with one mismatch (e.g., premium photography but discount-heavy copy). |
| 5–6 | Mixed signals — brand appears premium but prices suggest mass market, or vice versa. |
| 1–4 | Significant brand-price dissonance that would confuse the target customer. |

## Common Patterns by Storefront Stage

### Early-Stage DTC (< $1M revenue signals)
Common issues: missing reviews, weak about story, generic homepage copy, no email capture.
High-impact fixes: add 10+ reviews to PDP, write a specific USP headline, install exit-intent offer.

### Mid-Market (signals: polished design, broad catalog, press mentions)
Common issues: conversion leakage on product pages, inconsistent copy quality across large catalog, underused social proof.
High-impact fixes: PDP copy audit, review count surfaced on collection pages, bundle offers.

### Premium/Luxury
Common issues: over-designed at expense of clarity, missing trust signals (feels too cool to show reviews), weak mobile experience.
High-impact fixes: surface editorial reviews from press, ensure mobile navigation is frictionless, add specific quality detail copy.
